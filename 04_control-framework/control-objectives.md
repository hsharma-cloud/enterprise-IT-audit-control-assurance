# Control Objectives

**Project:** enterprise-IT-audit-control-assurance
**Version:** 1.0
**Owner:** IT Audit Function

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to define control objectives that address identified risks within the IT environment. Control objectives describe the intended outcomes that controls must achieve to mitigate risks effectively.

---

## 2. Objectives

* Establish clear control expectations
* Align controls with identified risks
* Provide a foundation for control design and testing
* Support consistent evaluation of control effectiveness

---

## 3. Control Objective Definition

A control objective defines:

> What must be achieved to mitigate a specific risk.

It does not describe how the control is implemented, but rather the expected outcome.

---

## 4. Control Objectives by Domain

---

### 4.1 Identity and Access Control

* Access to systems and data must be restricted to authorized users only
* Users must be granted access based on least privilege principles
* Authentication mechanisms must be secure and enforced (e.g., MFA)

---

### 4.2 User Lifecycle Management

* User accounts must be provisioned only with proper authorization
* Access must be removed promptly upon termination or role change
* Periodic access reviews must be conducted

---

### 4.3 Privileged Access Management

* Privileged access must be strictly controlled and limited
* All privileged activities must be logged and monitored
* Privileged access must be reviewed regularly

---

### 4.4 Logging and Monitoring

* Security events must be logged and retained
* Logs must be monitored for suspicious activity
* Alerts must be generated and investigated

---

### 4.5 Network Security

* Network access must be controlled and segmented
* Traffic must be monitored and filtered
* Secure communication protocols must be enforced

---

### 4.6 Vulnerability and Patch Management

* Systems must be regularly scanned for vulnerabilities
* Patches must be applied in a timely manner
* Exceptions must be documented and approved

---

### 4.7 Change and Configuration Management

* Changes must be authorized, tested, and approved
* Configurations must align with secure baselines
* Unauthorized changes must be detected

---

### 4.8 Data Protection

* Sensitive data must be classified and protected
* Data must be encrypted in transit and at rest
* Access to data must be restricted and monitored

---

## 5. Mapping to Risks

Each control objective must be mapped to one or more risks identified in the risk register to ensure full coverage.

---

## 6. Evaluation Criteria

Control objectives are considered effective when:

* Controls are implemented to meet the objective
* Controls are consistently operating as intended
* Risks are reduced to acceptable levels

---

## 7. Usage

Control objectives are used to:

* Design control frameworks
* Develop audit procedures
* Evaluate control effectiveness

---

## 8. Conclusion

Control objectives provide a clear link between identified risks and the controls required to mitigate them, forming the foundation for effective audit and assurance activities.
