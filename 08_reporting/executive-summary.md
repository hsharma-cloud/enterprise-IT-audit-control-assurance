## 1. Overview

An audit of the defined IT environment was conducted to evaluate the design and effectiveness of key controls. The audit focused on areas including identity and access management, network security, vulnerability management, and security operations.

The objective was to assess whether controls are adequately designed, implemented, and operating effectively to mitigate identified risks.

---

## 2. Scope

The audit covered:

* Systems and applications within the defined audit scope
* Identity and access management processes
* Security monitoring and logging controls
* Vulnerability and patch management processes

The audit period included activities performed during the defined timeframe.

---

## 3. Key Findings

The audit identified several control strengths as well as areas requiring improvement.

### Strengths

* Multi-factor authentication is implemented and enforced
* Centralized logging and monitoring are in place
* Firewall controls are properly configured

### Areas for Improvement

* Excessive user permissions identified in some systems
* Delays in user deprovisioning processes
* Inconsistent patch management across systems

---

## 4. Risk Summary

Findings were classified based on risk severity:

* **High Risk:** Delayed user deprovisioning
* **Medium Risk:** Excessive user permissions, inconsistent patching
* **Low Risk:** Minor control improvements

These risks may lead to unauthorized access, increased vulnerability exposure, and operational inefficiencies if not addressed.

---

## 5. Overall Audit Opinion

Based on the procedures performed:

> Controls are generally in place and functioning; however, certain control weaknesses were identified that require remediation to fully mitigate associated risks.

---

## 6. Recommendations

Key recommendations include:

* Strengthen role-based access controls and conduct periodic access reviews
* Automate user deprovisioning processes to ensure timely access removal
* Enhance patch management processes and enforce remediation timelines

---

## 7. Management Response

Management has acknowledged the findings and committed to implementing remediation actions within defined timelines.

---

## 8. Conclusion

The audit concludes that while the control environment is largely effective, improvements are necessary to enhance security posture and reduce risk exposure.

Timely remediation of identified findings will strengthen overall control effectiveness and ensure alignment with enterprise security standards.
