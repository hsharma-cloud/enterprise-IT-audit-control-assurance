## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to define detailed audit procedures and testing steps to evaluate the effectiveness of controls within the audit scope.

---

## 2. Audit Approach

The audit will be conducted using a structured, risk-based approach, including:

* Understanding the control environment
* Identifying key controls
* Performing control testing
* Collecting and validating evidence
* Documenting results and findings

---

## 3. Control Testing Overview

Each control will be evaluated using:

* **Test of Design (ToD):** Assess whether the control is appropriately designed
* **Test of Operating Effectiveness (ToE):** Assess whether the control operates consistently over time

---

## 4. Audit Procedures

---

### 4.1 Access Control Testing

**Objective:** Ensure only authorized users have access to systems

**Procedures:**

* Obtain user access listings from systems
* Review user roles and permissions
* Verify access approvals for a sample of users
* Identify inactive or orphaned accounts

**Evidence:**

* User access reports
* Approval records
* System screenshots

---

### 4.2 User Lifecycle Testing

**Objective:** Validate proper provisioning and deprovisioning of user accounts

**Procedures:**

* Select a sample of new, modified, and terminated users
* Verify account creation approvals
* Confirm timely removal of access for terminated users
* Review role changes and approvals

**Evidence:**

* HR records
* Access request tickets
* System logs

---

### 4.3 Privileged Access Testing

**Objective:** Ensure privileged accounts are controlled and monitored

**Procedures:**

* Obtain list of privileged accounts
* Verify assignment and approval of privileged access
* Review usage logs of privileged accounts
* Confirm periodic access reviews

**Evidence:**

* Privileged access reports
* Audit logs
* Access review documentation

---

### 4.4 Logging and Monitoring Testing

**Objective:** Ensure logging and monitoring controls are effective

**Procedures:**

* Verify logging is enabled for key systems
* Review log retention settings
* Examine alerts for suspicious activities
* Validate incident response actions

**Evidence:**

* Log samples
* SIEM alerts
* Incident tickets

---

### 4.5 Policy Compliance Testing

**Objective:** Ensure adherence to defined policies and standards

**Procedures:**

* Review applicable policies and standards
* Compare system configurations against standards
* Identify deviations or non-compliance

**Evidence:**

* Policy documents
* Configuration files
* Compliance reports

---

## 5. Sampling Methodology

Sampling will be used where full population testing is not feasible.

* Define sample size based on risk and population size
* Use random or judgmental sampling techniques
* Ensure samples are representative

---

## 6. Documentation of Results

For each test performed:

* Document procedures executed
* Record results and observations
* Link evidence to test steps
* Identify exceptions or deviations

---

## 7. Exception Handling

* Document all control failures or deviations
* Assess impact and likelihood
* Escalate significant issues

---

## 8. Quality Review

All audit work must undergo review to ensure:

* Accuracy and completeness
* Adequate supporting evidence
* Alignment with audit objectives

---

## 9. Deliverables

* Completed audit program with test results
* Supporting evidence repository
* Identified findings and recommendations

---

## 10. Conclusion

The audit program provides a structured approach to evaluate controls and ensure consistent execution of audit procedures across engagements.
