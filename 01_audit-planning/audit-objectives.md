## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to define the specific objectives of the audit engagement. These objectives guide audit procedures and ensure that testing is aligned with identified risks and control requirements.

---

## 2. Overall Objective

To evaluate whether controls within the defined audit area are:

* Properly designed to mitigate identified risks
* Implemented in accordance with policies and standards
* Operating effectively over time

---

## 3. Control Objectives

The audit will assess the following control objectives:

---

### 3.1 Access Control

* Ensure that access to systems and data is restricted to authorized users
* Verify implementation of least privilege principles
* Confirm proper authentication and authorization mechanisms

---

### 3.2 User Lifecycle Management

* Validate that user accounts are provisioned, modified, and deprovisioned appropriately
* Ensure timely removal of access for terminated or transferred users
* Confirm approval processes for access requests

---

### 3.3 Privileged Access Management

* Ensure privileged accounts are identified and controlled
* Verify use of elevated access controls and monitoring
* Confirm periodic review of privileged access

---

### 3.4 Logging and Monitoring

* Ensure security events are logged and retained
* Verify monitoring of access and administrative activities
* Confirm alerting and response mechanisms are in place

---

### 3.5 Policy and Compliance

* Validate alignment with internal policies and standards
* Ensure compliance with applicable regulatory requirements
* Confirm enforcement of security controls

---

## 4. Risk Alignment

Each objective is aligned to key risks, including:

* Unauthorized access to systems or data
* Excessive or inappropriate privileges
* Lack of visibility into user activities
* Delayed or ineffective access removal
* Non-compliance with security policies

---

## 5. Success Criteria

The audit objectives are considered achieved when:

* Controls are appropriately designed and documented
* Controls are consistently operating as intended
* No significant control deficiencies are identified
* Risks are effectively mitigated

---

## 6. Link to Audit Procedures

All audit procedures and test steps will be developed to directly support these objectives. Each test must map to at least one control objective defined in this document.

---

## 7. Assumptions

* Controls are implemented within the audit scope
* Documentation and system access are available
* Stakeholders will provide required information
