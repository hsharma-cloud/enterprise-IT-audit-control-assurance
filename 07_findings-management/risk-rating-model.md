# Risk Rating Model

**Project:** enterprise-IT-audit-control-assurance
**Version:** 1.0
**Owner:** IT Audit Function

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to define a standardized approach for rating the severity of audit findings. This ensures consistency, transparency, and risk-based prioritization of remediation efforts.

---

## 2. Objectives

* Establish a consistent method for evaluating risk
* Classify findings based on severity
* Support prioritization of remediation actions
* Provide clear communication to stakeholders

---

## 3. Risk Rating Components

Risk ratings are determined based on:

### 3.1 Impact

The potential consequence to the organization if the risk materializes.

### 3.2 Likelihood

The probability that the risk will occur.

---

## 4. Impact Criteria

| Level | Description                        |
| ----- | ---------------------------------- |
| 1     | Insignificant – minimal impact     |
| 2     | Minor – limited operational impact |
| 3     | Moderate – noticeable impact       |
| 4     | Major – significant disruption     |
| 5     | Critical – severe business impact  |

---

## 5. Likelihood Criteria

| Level | Description                          |
| ----- | ------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Rare – unlikely to occur             |
| 2     | Unlikely – low probability           |
| 3     | Possible – could occur occasionally  |
| 4     | Likely – expected to occur           |
| 5     | Almost Certain – frequent occurrence |

---

## 6. Risk Scoring Formula

Risk Score = Impact × Likelihood

---

## 7. Risk Rating Levels

| Score Range | Risk Level |
| ----------- | ---------- |
| 1–5         | Low        |
| 6–12        | Medium     |
| 13–25       | High       |

---

## 8. Risk Rating Matrix

| Impact \ Likelihood | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
| ------------------- | - | - | - | - | - |
| 5 (Critical)        | M | M | H | H | H |
| 4 (Major)           | M | M | M | H | H |
| 3 (Moderate)        | L | M | M | M | H |
| 2 (Minor)           | L | L | M | M | M |
| 1 (Insignificant)   | L | L | L | M | M |

(L = Low, M = Medium, H = High)

---

## 9. Risk Rating Guidelines

### High Risk

* Immediate remediation required
* Significant impact to business or security

### Medium Risk

* Remediation required within defined timeframe
* Moderate impact

### Low Risk

* Minor issue
* Improvement recommended

---

## 10. Application of Risk Ratings

Risk ratings are applied to:

* Audit findings
* Control deficiencies
* Identified risks

---

## 11. Review and Approval

Risk ratings must be:

* Reviewed by audit team
* Validated for consistency
* Approved before final reporting

---

## 12. Conclusion

A standardized risk rating model ensures that audit findings are evaluated consistently and supports effective risk management and decision-making.
