# Root Cause Analysis

**Project:** enterprise-IT-audit-control-assurance
**Version:** 1.0
**Owner:** IT Audit Function

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to identify and analyze the underlying causes of audit findings. Understanding root causes ensures that remediation efforts address the source of issues rather than just the symptoms.

---

## 2. Objectives

* Identify the fundamental causes of control deficiencies
* Support effective and sustainable remediation
* Prevent recurrence of similar issues
* Improve overall control environment

---

## 3. Root Cause Definition

A root cause is:

> The underlying reason a control deficiency exists or a control failure occurred.

---

## 4. Root Cause Categories

Root causes are categorized as follows:

### 4.1 Process Deficiency

* Lack of defined procedures
* Ineffective workflows
* Missing or incomplete processes

---

### 4.2 Control Design Weakness

* Controls are not adequately designed
* Controls do not fully mitigate risks

---

### 4.3 Control Execution Failure

* Controls exist but are not consistently performed
* Human error or lack of enforcement

---

### 4.4 Technology Limitation

* System limitations or misconfigurations
* Lack of automation or integration

---

### 4.5 Governance and Oversight

* Lack of monitoring or supervision
* Inadequate management review

---

### 4.6 Training and Awareness

* Lack of user awareness
* Insufficient training on processes or controls

---

---

## 6. Analysis Approach

Root cause analysis is performed by:

1. Reviewing audit findings and evidence
2. Conducting interviews with stakeholders
3. Analyzing process and control gaps
4. Identifying patterns across findings

---

## 7. Impact of Root Causes

Unresolved root causes may lead to:

* Recurring control failures
* Increased risk exposure
* Ineffective remediation efforts

---

## 8. Link to Remediation

Each root cause must be directly linked to remediation actions that:

* Address the underlying issue
* Improve control design or execution
* Strengthen governance and oversight

---

## 9. Continuous Improvement

Root cause analysis supports:

* Process optimization
* Control enhancements
* Long-term risk reduction

---

## 10. Conclusion

Identifying and addressing root causes ensures that audit findings lead to meaningful and lasting improvements in the control environment.
