# Control Testing Strategy

**Project:** enterprise-IT-audit-control-assurance
**Version:** 1.0
**Owner:** IT Audit Function

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to define the strategy for testing controls within the audit scope. It establishes a consistent approach for evaluating control design and operating effectiveness.

---

## 2. Objectives

* Define a structured approach to control testing
* Ensure consistency across audit engagements
* Determine appropriate testing methods and depth
* Support reliable and evidence-based conclusions

---

## 3. Testing Approach

Control testing will be performed using a risk-based approach:

* High-risk controls → Extensive testing
* Medium-risk controls → Moderate testing
* Low-risk controls → Limited or sample-based testing

---

## 4. Types of Testing

### 4.1 Test of Design (ToD)

Evaluates whether the control is appropriately designed to mitigate the identified risk.

**Focus:**

* Control existence
* Alignment with control objectives
* Adequacy of design

---

### 4.2 Test of Operating Effectiveness (ToE)

Evaluates whether the control is operating consistently over time.

**Focus:**

* Frequency of control execution
* Consistency of performance
* Evidence of operation

---

## 5. Testing Techniques

The following techniques will be used:

### 5.1 Inquiry

* Interviews with control owners and stakeholders

### 5.2 Observation

* Direct observation of control execution

### 5.3 Inspection

* Review of documents, logs, and configurations

### 5.4 Re-performance

* Independent execution of control procedures

---

## 6. Sampling Strategy

Sampling will be applied where full population testing is not feasible.

* Sample size determined by risk level and population size
* Random or judgmental sampling methods used
* Samples must be representative of the population

---

## 7. Evidence Requirements

All testing must be supported by sufficient and appropriate evidence:

* System-generated logs
* Screenshots
* Configuration files
* Approval records

Evidence must be:

* Accurate
* Complete
* Traceable

---

## 8. Evaluation Criteria

Controls will be evaluated based on:

* Design Effectiveness
* Operating Effectiveness

Results will be categorized as:

* Effective
* Partially Effective
* Ineffective

---

## 9. Exception Handling

Exceptions identified during testing must be:

* Documented clearly
* Evaluated for impact and likelihood
* Linked to associated risks

Significant exceptions will be escalated.

---

## 10. Documentation Standards

All testing activities must include:

* Test procedures performed
* Evidence collected
* Results and conclusions
* Identified exceptions

---

## 11. Quality Assurance

Testing results will be reviewed to ensure:

* Completeness and accuracy
* Adequate supporting evidence
* Alignment with audit objectives

---

## 12. Conclusion

The control testing strategy ensures a consistent, risk-based, and evidence-driven approach to evaluating the effectiveness of controls within the enterprise.

---
