# Risk Assessment Methodology

**Project:** enterprise-IT-audit-control-assurance
**Version:** 1.0
**Owner:** IT Audit Function

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to define the methodology used to identify, assess, and prioritize risks within the IT environment. This ensures a consistent and risk-based approach to audit planning and execution.

---

## 2. Objectives

The objectives of the risk assessment methodology are to:

* Identify risks associated with IT systems and processes
* Evaluate the likelihood and impact of risks
* Prioritize risks for audit focus
* Support risk-based decision-making

---

## 3. Risk Definition

Risk is defined as:

> The potential for a threat to exploit a vulnerability, resulting in an adverse impact to the organization.

---

## 4. Risk Components

Risk is evaluated based on the following components:

### 4.1 Threat

A potential event that could cause harm (e.g., cyber attack, insider misuse)

### 4.2 Vulnerability

A weakness that can be exploited (e.g., misconfiguration, lack of controls)

### 4.3 Impact

The potential consequence to the organization (e.g., financial loss, data breach)

### 4.4 Likelihood

The probability that a risk event will occur

---

## 5. Risk Assessment Process

The risk assessment process includes the following steps:

1. Identify assets and systems
2. Identify threats and vulnerabilities
3. Assess impact and likelihood
4. Determine risk level
5. Document risks in the risk register

---

## 6. Risk Scoring Model

Risk is calculated using the following formula:

Risk Score = Likelihood × Impact

---

### 6.1 Likelihood Scale

| Score | Description    |
| ----- | -------------- |
| 1     | Rare           |
| 2     | Unlikely       |
| 3     | Possible       |
| 4     | Likely         |
| 5     | Almost Certain |

---

### 6.2 Impact Scale

| Score | Description   |
| ----- | ------------- |
| 1     | Insignificant |
| 2     | Minor         |
| 3     | Moderate      |
| 4     | Major         |
| 5     | Critical      |

---

### 6.3 Risk Rating

| Score Range | Risk Level |
| ----------- | ---------- |
| 1–5         | Low        |
| 6–12        | Medium     |
| 13–25       | High       |

---

## 7. Inherent vs Residual Risk

### 7.1 Inherent Risk

Risk level before considering existing controls

### 7.2 Residual Risk

Risk remaining after controls are applied

---

## 8. Risk Prioritization

Risks are prioritized based on:

* Risk score
* Business criticality
* Data sensitivity
* Regulatory impact

---

## 9. Risk Documentation

All identified risks must be documented in a risk register, including:

* Risk description
* Associated assets/systems
* Likelihood and impact scores
* Risk rating
* Existing controls
* Residual risk

---

## 10. Review and Updates

The risk assessment must be:

* Reviewed periodically
* Updated based on changes in systems or threats
* Aligned with audit planning

---

## 11. Conclusion

A consistent risk assessment methodology enables effective prioritization of audit efforts and ensures focus on areas of highest risk.
