# Risk Register

**Project:** enterprise-IT-audit-control-assurance
**Version:** 1.0
**Owner:** IT Audit Function

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of the Risk Register is to document identified risks, assess their severity, and track mitigation through controls. It serves as a central repository for risk management and audit prioritization.

---

## 2. Risk Register Structure

Each risk entry includes:

* Risk ID
* Risk Description
* Affected Asset/System
* Threat and Vulnerability
* Likelihood
* Impact
* Risk Score
* Risk Level
* Existing Controls
* Residual Risk
* Owner

## 4. Risk Analysis

### 4.1 High Risks

* Require immediate attention and audit focus
* Typically involve critical systems and high-impact scenarios

### 4.2 Medium Risks

* Require monitoring and scheduled remediation
* Included in periodic audit reviews

### 4.3 Low Risks

* Minor impact
* Addressed as part of continuous improvement

---

## 5. Residual Risk Evaluation

Residual risk is determined after considering existing controls. If residual risk remains high, additional controls or remediation actions are required.

---

## 6. Risk Ownership

Each risk must have a designated owner responsible for:

* Managing the risk
* Implementing controls
* Monitoring risk status

---

## 7. Maintenance

The risk register must be:

* Updated regularly
* Reviewed during audit planning
* Aligned with changes in systems and processes

---

## 8. Integration with Audit Activities

The risk register is used to:

* Prioritize audit engagements
* Define audit objectives
* Guide control testing

---

## 9. Conclusion

The Risk Register provides a structured approach to identifying, assessing, and managing risks, ensuring that audit efforts are focused on areas of highest importance.
