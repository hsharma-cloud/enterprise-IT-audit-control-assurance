# IT Audit Methodology

**Project:** enterprise-IT-audit-control-assurance
**Version:** 1.0
**Owner:** IT Audit Function

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the standardized methodology for conducting IT audits within the enterprise. It ensures audits are executed in a consistent, risk-based, and evidence-driven manner to evaluate the design and operating effectiveness of IT controls.

---

## 2. Objectives

The objectives of this methodology are to:

* Provide a structured approach to IT audit engagements
* Evaluate whether controls are properly designed and implemented
* Assess whether controls operate effectively over time
* Identify control deficiencies and associated risks
* Support management in improving control environments

---

## 3. Scope

This methodology applies to all IT audit activities, including:

* Infrastructure and platform environments
* Identity and access management
* Network security controls
* Security operations and monitoring
* Change and configuration management
* Data protection and backup processes
* Cloud and hybrid environments

---

## 4. Audit Principles

All audit engagements adhere to the following principles:

### 4.1 Independence

Audit activities must remain independent from operational responsibilities.

### 4.2 Objectivity

All conclusions must be based on verifiable evidence and unbiased analysis.

### 4.3 Risk-Based Approach

Audit focus is determined by risk exposure and business impact.

### 4.4 Evidence-Based Validation

All findings must be supported by documented and traceable evidence.

### 4.5 Consistency and Repeatability

Audit procedures must be standardized and reproducible across engagements.

---

## 5. Audit Lifecycle

The IT audit lifecycle consists of the following phases:

---

### 5.1 Planning

* Define audit objectives and scope
* Identify stakeholders and control owners
* Develop audit procedures and timelines
* Perform preliminary risk assessment

**Deliverables:**

* Audit Charter
* Audit Scope
* Audit Program

---

### 5.2 Entity Understanding

* Review system architecture and data flows
* Understand business processes and dependencies
* Identify key systems and control environments

**Deliverables:**

* System Architecture Overview
* Process Documentation

---

### 5.3 Risk Assessment

* Identify threats and vulnerabilities
* Evaluate inherent and residual risks
* Prioritize audit focus areas

**Deliverables:**

* Risk Register
* Risk Assessment Summary

---

### 5.4 Control Identification

* Define control objectives
* Map controls to identified risks
* Identify key controls for testing

**Deliverables:**

* Control Matrix
* Control Mapping

---

### 5.5 Control Testing

Control testing is performed in two phases:

#### Test of Design (ToD)

Evaluate whether the control is appropriately designed to mitigate the identified risk.

#### Test of Operating Effectiveness (ToE)

Assess whether the control is consistently operating as intended over a defined period.

Testing techniques include:

* Inquiry
* Observation
* Inspection
* Re-performance

**Deliverables:**

* Test Procedures
* Test Results

---

### 5.6 Evidence Collection

* Collect supporting evidence such as logs, screenshots, and configurations
* Maintain traceability between controls and evidence
* Ensure evidence integrity and completeness

**Deliverables:**

* Evidence Repository
* Supporting Documentation

---

### 5.7 Findings Development

* Identify control deficiencies
* Perform root cause analysis
* Assign risk ratings based on impact and likelihood

**Deliverables:**

* Findings Log
* Risk Ratings

---

### 5.8 Reporting

* Prepare clear and actionable audit reports
* Include management responses and remediation plans
* Provide an overall audit opinion

**Deliverables:**

* Executive Summary
* Detailed Audit Report

---

### 5.9 Follow-Up

* Validate remediation actions
* Confirm closure of findings
* Update audit records

**Deliverables:**

* Remediation Validation Report
* Closure Report

---

## 6. Control Evaluation Criteria

Controls are evaluated based on:

* **Design Effectiveness:** Ability of the control to mitigate risk
* **Operating Effectiveness:** Consistency of control execution over time

---

## 7. Risk Rating Model

Findings are categorized as:

* **High Risk:** Significant impact requiring immediate remediation
* **Medium Risk:** Moderate impact requiring timely remediation
* **Low Risk:** Minor impact with recommended improvements

Risk ratings consider:

* Likelihood of occurrence
* Business impact
* Control maturity

---

## 8. Documentation Standards

All audit work must be documented to ensure:

* Full traceability from risk → control → test → evidence → finding
* Consistency across audit engagements
* Readiness for internal and external review

---

## 9. Quality Assurance

Audit engagements are subject to quality review to ensure:

* Compliance with methodology
* Accuracy and completeness of findings
* Adequate supporting evidence

---

## 10. Continuous Improvement

This methodology will be periodically reviewed and updated based on:

* Changes in technology and risk landscape
* Lessons learned from prior audits
* Evolving industry practices
